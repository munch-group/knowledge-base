**KANSL1** (I95, rs4792830, π>0.99) — this is the one to look at closely. KANSL1 sits inside the 17q21.31 **MAPT** inversion you already have in [KB:mn9z13p244y8]. It's in the NSL complex with KAT8/MOF (primarily H4K16ac, though KAT8 has non-histone substrates). The selection signal here is essentially capturing the H1/H2 inversion haplotype rather than KANSL1 function per se meaning this locus is picking up post-Neolithic selection on the same inversion that carries MAPT. That seems worth connecting to your Neanderthal/tau work.

The classical story was "H2 is under positive selection in Europeans" (Stefansson 2005). More recent structural work (Steinberg et al. 2012; Boettger et al. 2012) and a very recent Eichler-lab ancient-DNA reanalysis (bioRxiv Aug 2025, Porubsky et al.) reframes it:

Four structural classes segregate: H1.β1 (direct, no KANSL1 duplication — the reference), H1.β2+/H1D (direct, with β-duplication of KANSL1), H2.α1/H2′ (inverted, no duplication — ancestral form, still present in Khoisan), and H2.α2+/H2D (inverted, with α-duplication of KANSL1).
The Porubsky ancient-DNA reconstruction shows H1.β1 declined from >90% to ~60% over the last 11 kyr, while H1.β2+ rose from ~1% to ~29% and H2.α2+ rose from ~7% to ~21%. The two duplicated forms swept together, on both inversion backgrounds.
The Irving-Pease 2024 paper (the only one in your Table S6.1 to hit this locus) explicitly notes the duplications on H1 and H2 "probably occurred independently… and both are found in high frequencies (15–25%) among current European and Middle Eastern populations."

What this means for your interpretation:

If you want the "which haplotype is rising" answer, it's both duplicated forms (H1.β2+ and H2.α2+) at the expense of ancestral H1.β1.
A single tag SNP like rs4792830 will give you a blurred signal — it will partially track H2 (if C is the H2 allele, as expected), but the real causal unit is KANSL1 copy number, which is not captured by any single SNP.
If this matters for your KANSL1-as-MT-regulator framing: the selection is on more KANSL1 (via duplication), independently on both inversion backgrounds. That's a different story than "H2 is favored" and more consistent with a dosage effect on the NSL complex.
