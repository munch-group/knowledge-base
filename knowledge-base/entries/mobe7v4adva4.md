**KANSL1** (I95, rs4792830, π>0.99) — this is the one to look at closely. KANSL1 sits inside the 17q21.31 **MAPT** inversion you already have in [KB:mn9z13p244y8]. It's in the NSL complex with KAT8/MOF (primarily H4K16ac, though KAT8 has non-histone substrates). The selection signal here is essentially capturing the H1/H2 inversion haplotype rather than KANSL1 function per se meaning this locus is picking up post-Neolithic selection on the same inversion that carries MAPT. That seems worth connecting to your Neanderthal/tau work.

**CSK** (I80, rs1378942, π>0.99) — C-terminal Src kinase, the main negative regulator of **FYN** (and other SFKs). FYN is on your tau-kinase list in [KB:mna0610oovuf], so CSK is one step upstream of tau phosphorylation regulation. Not a microtubule gene itself.
